# Formula Laboratory

Formula Laboratory is the third built-in guided campaign. It teaches semantic comparison of Formula A and Formula B without treating a finite collection of examples as a proof of general equivalence.

The eight missions cover modal duality, valid distribution laws, failed converses, separate diamond witnesses, modal order, and the serial-frame implication `□p → ◇p`.

Every comparison mission displays stable Formula A/B labels and may require an exact truth pair at the evaluation world. This lets a mission ask for `A` true and `B` false, rather than merely detecting that the formulas differ.

Reference constructions are tested using the same evaluator as the workspace. Where a debrief says a relationship is generally valid, that claim is explained by the semantic clauses; a single finite model is only an illustration.
